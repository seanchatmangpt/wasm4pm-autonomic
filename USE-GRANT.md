# Permitted Use Guide: Compiled Cognition

This document explains in plain language what you can and cannot do with dteam under the Business Source License 1.1.

---

## What You CAN Do (No Permission Needed)

You may use dteam freely for:

- **Study and Learning**: Read the source code, understand how it works, use it to learn about compiled cognition, Petri nets, process mining, or deterministic AI
- **Research**: Academic research, papers, dissertations, conference presentations
- **Education**: Teaching, coursework, learning materials
- **Personal Projects**: Build tools for yourself, experiment, prototype
- **Internal Development**: Use within your organization for internal tools, testing, experimentation
- **Non-Production Use**: Dev environments, CI/CD, testing, training
- **Small Organizations**: If your organization has less than $1,000,000 annual revenue AND you're not offering dteam as a service to others, you may use it for production

**In short: if you're learning, researching, building for yourself, or running a small operation internally, you're fine.**

---

## What You CANNOT Do (Requires Commercial License)

You may NOT use dteam without a commercial license for:

- **Hosted Services**: Offering dteam as a hosted/managed/cloud service
- **Platform Products**: Building an "AI decision engine as a service" using dteam
- **Competitive Services**: Offering something that competes with dteam's commercial offering by embedding dteam
- **Embedding in Commercial Products**: Shipping dteam as part of a product you sell
- **Resale or Sublicensing**: Selling access to dteam or its outputs
- **Per-Inference Revenue**: Charging customers for decisions made by dteam
- **Platform Capture**: Creating a "dteam Cloud" or similar dependency-rent model

**In short: if you're building a business around dteam as the core product/service, you need a commercial license.**

---

## The Principle: Blue River Dam Economics

Compiled Cognition exists to **remove dependency tax** — the cost of relying on an external AI service.

The license prevents a scenario where:
1. Someone takes the open-source dteam
2. Wraps it in a managed cloud service
3. Charges per decision (or per deployment, or per audit)
4. Recreates the exact dependency tax the invention was designed to eliminate

This is called the **Blue River Dam pattern** — a free, public infrastructure becomes enclosed and monetized by a dominant player, strangling civilization benefit.
