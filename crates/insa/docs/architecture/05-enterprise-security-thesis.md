# INSA Enterprise Security Convergence Thesis

## The Board-Level Problem
**Your company does not have one security problem. It has disconnected security fields that no one can prove are consistent.**

Current security stacks are built around detection, not closure:
* SIEM detects events.
* IAM manages access.
* Badge systems track physical doors.
* HR tracks employment.
* Cloud tools track posture.

But catastrophic risk lives in the intersections:
`Identity AND Badge AND Device AND Policy AND Site AND Time AND Vendor AND Asset`

## The INSA Solution: Converged Security Closure
INSA builds a graph-backed security closure layer across all enterprise fields. It continuously detects where the fields do not close.

**A workflow engine routes tasks; POWL8 proves that motion itself is lawful.**

### The Capabilities
1. **Access Drift & Field Mismatch Closure:** Detect when human, physical, and cyber access no longer agree (e.g., Terminated contractor drift).
2. **Release-Gate Security Closure:** Prevent deployment if critical vulnerabilities are reachable, exposed, and unowned.
3. **Material Incident Clock:** Prove what was known, when, and preserve evidence routes for SEC/NIST compliance.

### Why Byte-Speed is Mandatory
At Fortune 500 scale, byte-speed is not about going faster. It is about making closure cheap enough to happen everywhere, continuously, before failure becomes work.

Little Law dictates that if the time to close an item is too high, unresolved risk explodes. LLM agents often increase the arrival rate of work. INSA suppresses the arrival rate of wrong work.
