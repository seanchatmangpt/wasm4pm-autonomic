# Commercial Licensing: Compiled Cognition

Compiled Cognition is released under BUSL 1.1 with a 4-year time limit to openness. For organizations that need to use dteam before April 18, 2029 in ways not permitted by the free grant, commercial licenses are available.

---

## Who Needs a Commercial License?

You need a commercial license if any of these apply:

1. **Your organization has >$1M annual revenue** and you want to use dteam in production
2. **You want to embed dteam** in a commercial product you sell to others
3. **You want to offer a hosted or managed service** using dteam (e.g., "Compiled Cognition Cloud")
4. **You want to resell or sublicense** dteam or its outputs
5. **Your primary business is providing dteam-based AI services** to external customers

---

## What a Commercial License Includes

A commercial license from dteam permits you to:

- Use dteam in production for commercial purposes
- Embed dteam in products you distribute to customers
- Offer hosted or managed services based on dteam
- Sublicense to end-users under your own terms (subject to mission covenant)
- Access commercial support, updates, and maintenance
- Obtain written patent license coverage
- Certification for regulatory/compliance readiness

---

## What a Commercial License Does NOT Include

A commercial license does **not** permit:

- **Per-inference revenue models**: You may not charge customers per decision/inference made by dteam
- **Dependency-rent recreation**: You may not rebuild the dependency tax Compiled Cognition removes
- **Exclusive distribution**: You remain non-exclusive; we continue to license others
- **Modification of the source**: Modifications still fall under BUSL until Change Date
- **Competing patents**: We retain our patent rights; your license doesn't grant you our IP

---

## Mission Covenant

Every commercial license includes a **Mission Covenant**:

> You may use this to **remove dams, not to build a larger dam downstream.**

Specifically, commercial licensees agree:

1. **No per-inference pricing**: You will not charge your customers per decision/inference
2. **No dependency lock-in**: You will not create artificial switching costs or lock-in around dteam
3. **Transparency on licensing**: You will disclose to your customers that their system uses Compiled Cognition
4. **No hostile licensing**: You will not use dteam to create a competing closed product after obtaining our IP

Examples of **acceptable commercial use:**
- Selling a product that uses dteam as one component
- Offering consulting/implementation services around dteam deployment
- Providing a managed hosting service with transparent pricing
- Building an enterprise analytics platform that includes dteam
- Training employees or partners on Compiled Cognition

Examples of **unacceptable commercial use** (forbidden):
- "Compiled Cognition Cloud" charging $0.001 per inference
- Licensing dteam's pre-compiled models as a black-box API service
- Embedding dteam and claiming it's proprietary
- Using dteam to replace an open-source product with a closed alternative

## License Term & Renewal

**Standard commercial license:**
- **Term**: 1 year (renewable annually)
- **Renewal**: Automatic unless either party opts out
- **Termination for cause**: Material breach of Mission Covenant; notice period 30 days, opportunity to cure 14 days
- **Survival**: After April 18, 2029, your commercial license expires and dteam becomes Apache 2.0; you may continue using it under Apache 2.0

## Transition to April 18, 2029

**What happens on the Change Date?**

On April 18, 2029, dteam automatically converts to Apache License 2.0. Your commercial license **expires** on that date, but you gain an **automatic Apache 2.0 license** at no cost.

**Your options:**
1. **Continue under Apache 2.0** with no license fee (recommended for most users)
2. **Request a license extension** if you have specific commercial terms worth preserving
3. **Stay under BUSL** if you prefer the original restrictions (we can grant an extension on request)

**Most users will simply transition to Apache 2.0 and continue using dteam freely.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will you keep charging after April 18, 2029?**

A: No. On April 18, 2029, dteam becomes Apache 2.0. All commercial licenses expire. You can use dteam freely under Apache 2.0 thereafter.

**Q: Can you extend the Change Date?**

A: We will not extend the Change Date unilaterally. If you need a custom arrangement, contact us; we can discuss a separate agreement. But the default path is: April 18, 2029 → Apache 2.0.

**Q: What if I want exclusive rights?**

A: We do not grant exclusive licenses. Compiled Cognition must remain available for broad use to prevent platform capture.

**Q: Can I use dteam without a commercial license if I contribute back to open source?**

A: Contributions are welcome and encouraged. But the free grant is already very broad. If you're not sure whether you need a license, see `USE-GRANT.md` or contact us.

**Q: What if I run a nonprofit or educational organization?**

A: The free grant covers educational and nonprofit use. If you're above $1M revenue and offering dteam as a service, you need a commercial license. Otherwise, you're fine under the free grant.
